About Me

Hi! I'm an undergraduate student in Data Science and Big Data Technology, studying through the joint program between Minzu University of China and City University of Macau, currently conducting research at the General & Reliable Intelligence (GRIN) Lab at CityU Macau.

My research interests lie at the intersection of computer vision and medical artificial intelligence. I am particularly interested in developing vision-language models that can better understand medical images in clinically meaningful ways and integrate visual reasoning with diagnostic workflows.

At present, I am working on anatomy-aware hierarchical reasoning approaches for automated radiology report generation. Previously, I have explored skeleton-guided visual understanding combined with retrieval-augmented generation for healthcare scenarios, as well as hybrid metaheuristic methods for combinatorial optimization problems.

I am actively looking for research opportunities (RA/ intern / master-leading to PhD) in computer vision, medical multimodal learning, and vision-language models, with the goal of applying for PhD programs starting in Fall 2027. Feel free to reach out if our interests align — I'm always happy to chat about potential collaborations.
